Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile man of India

Aerospace scientist and 11th President of India

Here is a timeline of Dr. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduate in Physics, from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960 - Joins Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as scientist.
  • 1969 - Move to Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he helms India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1980 - Leads India's entry in the space club by putting the Rohini satellite in Earth's orbit with the first indigenous SLV-III.
  • 1980-1990 - Develops Prithvi and Agni missiles.
  • 1981 - Recieves the Padma Bhushan for his contribution to the field of science and technology.
  • 1990 - Honoured with Padma Vibhushan, India’s second highest civilian honour.
  • 1992-1999 - Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO.
  • 1997 - Honoured with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 - Spearheads the Pokhran mission, India conducts the Pokhran II nuclear tests with Dr. Kalam being one of the forces behind its success.
  • 1999-2001 - Serves as India’s Chief Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.
  • 2002-2007 - Elected as India's 11th President.During his five years in office, he was fondly called ‘The People’s President'.
  • 2012 - Launches 'What Can I Give Movement’, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption.
  • July 27,2015 - Passes away in Shillong, Meghalaya because of a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

"He made invaluable contribution in strengthening India’s defence & space capabilities. He will always remain an inspiration to every Indian."

--Indian Vice President M Venkaiah Naidu
